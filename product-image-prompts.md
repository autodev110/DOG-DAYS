# DOG DAYS Product Image Prompts

Use these as Nano Banana prompts for replacing the CSS product placeholders with real product imagery. Keep the images square or 4:5, product-forward, isolated, and quiet. Avoid models, busy scenes, horror styling, memes, and readable brand text unless the prompt explicitly calls for a small tag or label.

## Shared Direction

Use a clean studio product photograph with a faint pastel cloud backdrop, soft blue-white-pink daylight, hard black outline accents, and a slightly bootleg art-brand sensibility. The object should look like merchandise from a serious but quietly wrong clothing label. Keep the product centered, fully visible, and usable for an online store grid. No people, no hands, no third-party logos, no checkout symbols, no barcode numbers that need to be legible.

## Weather Hold Hoodie

Create a square e-commerce product image of a heavy black pullover hoodie, front-facing, neatly floating or laid flat, with a small powder-blue DOG DAYS chest emblem inspired by a howling dog silhouette under rounded block letters. Add subtle utilitarian details: a sewn weather-hold label near the hem, thick drawstrings, slightly oversized hood, matte cotton fleece texture. Background is a soft pastel cloudy sky studio backdrop, pale blue and pink, clean and airy. The hoodie should feel premium, wearable, and slightly official, like staff issue for a store operating under uncertain weather. High detail fabric texture, crisp edges, product fully visible, no model, no hands, no dramatic shadows.

## Blue Hour Tee

Create a square e-commerce product image of a powder-blue short sleeve t-shirt, front-facing, flat but dimensional, with a small black outlined DOG DAYS chest mark and a tiny white care label near the side seam. The shirt should look soft, midweight, and plausibly retail-ready. Add a restrained printed detail near the lower front: a small black rectangular operational stamp with minimal abstract lines, not readable text. Background is a clean pastel cloud studio, white-blue-pink, with soft daylight and faint product shadow. Make it calm, legitimate, art-brand, and slightly uncanny through the overly official label placement. No human model, no hanger, no extra props.

## Counter Cap

Create a square product photo of a low-profile cotton twill cap in faded butter yellow, front three-quarter view, with a powder-blue DOG DAYS patch on the front and a black stitched outline around the patch. Add a small black weather tag hanging from the back strap. The cap should feel like a real boutique merch item, lightly worn but clean, with visible stitching and curved brim. Place it on a flat white surface against a pastel cloud backdrop. Add one small institutional detail: a blank inventory sticker tucked under the brim. No people, no hands, no sports-team styling, no loud streetwear treatment.

## Never Understand Poster

Create a square product image showing a single archival art poster leaning slightly against a clean wall, not the existing gallery image copied exactly. The poster should feel related: grayscale retro-commercial surrealism, ocean horizon, mannequin-like studio object, soft smoke-like shapes, one controlled red word area, and large blank white typography spaces that can be edited later. Keep it tasteful and graphic, like a limited art print sold by a conceptual clothing brand. Add a thin black frame line and a small DOG DAYS archive label on the table below. Soft pastel cloud light in the room, product fully visible, no people, no offensive imagery, no horror.

## Weather Receipt Zine

Create a square product image of a small stapled zine lying open at a slight angle, cream paper, black ink, minimal receipt-like grids, weather symbols, order numbers, and empty ruled spaces. Include a powder-blue cover page partially visible with a black outlined howling dog mark and the title area left mostly blank or abstract. Add one loose receipt slip tucked inside. Background is a white tabletop with a faint pastel sky reflection. The zine should feel tactile, collectible, and quietly bureaucratic, like printed matter from a store that records weather as inventory. No readable long text, no hands, no clutter.

## Closed Store Tote

Create a square e-commerce product image of a natural canvas tote bag standing upright, front-facing, with sturdy handles and a simple powder-blue DOG DAYS graphic printed low on the bag. Add black outline details and a small sewn label that feels like an official store weather tag. The tote should look useful and real, but the graphic placement should feel slightly too low and procedural. Use a clean pastel cloud studio background, soft daylight, faint floor shadow. Include subtle wrinkles and canvas texture. No person, no contents spilling out, no lifestyle scene.

## Pending Sticker Sheet

Create a square product image of a glossy sticker sheet on a white surface, nine die-cut stickers arranged in a strict grid. Stickers use DOG DAYS colors: powder blue, black, white, muted yellow, small red accents. Designs include abstract howling dog silhouette, cloud shape, receipt box, weather label, arrow to sky, blank status tabs, and circular seal. Keep text minimal and mostly symbolic so it does not need to be readable. Make the sheet feel like a real product photographed for an online store, with crisp die-cut edges and subtle gloss reflections. Pastel cloud backdrop, no hands, no messy desk.

## Howl Department Long Sleeve

Create a square e-commerce product image of a white long sleeve shirt, front-facing and laid flat, with powder-blue sleeve prints and a small black outlined DOG DAYS department mark on the chest. The sleeves should have repeating abstract operational symbols, not readable text: small boxes, arrows, cloud marks, and dog silhouette fragments. Fabric should look soft but structured. Background is a clean pale cloud studio with subtle shadow. The item should feel like department-issued merch from a strange art store, wearable and calm, not costume-like. No human model, no hanger, no loud graphics.

## Unclaimed Key Tag

Create a square macro product photo of a hard enamel key tag with a split ring, deep muted green enamel, black outline, and powder-blue DOG DAYS mark. The tag shape is vertical with a rounded top and small hole, like a hotel or weather desk key tag. Add a tiny blank white inventory sticker on the back edge and a small metal ring with realistic reflections. Place it on a clean white counter with pastel cloud colors softly reflected. The object should feel useful, official, and unresolved. No keys attached, no hands, no background clutter, no readable serial number required.

## Sky Return Pin Set

Create a square product image of three enamel pins arranged in a precise triangle on a white display card. Pins use powder blue, black, white, and one small red accent. Designs: a howling dog silhouette, a cloud/receipt hybrid, and an upward return arrow. The display card should have subtle DOG DAYS retail packaging style: black border, small blank label spaces, official but empty. Use pastel cloud studio lighting, crisp enamel highlights, realistic metal pin edges. The set should feel collectible and serious, with quiet absurdism in the symbols. No hands, no jewelry styling, no busy props.
